# Teaching Mixed Fractions – Class 6 (CBSE)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand the meaning of mixed fractions
- Convert improper fractions to mixed fractions and vice versa
- Represent mixed fractions on a number line

---

## 1. Slow Learners
### Objective
- Recognize and understand mixed fractions using real-life examples

### Teaching Strategy
- Use visuals (chapati, chocolate, fruits)
- Simple language and repetition

### Explanation
A mixed fraction has:
- A **whole number**
- A **proper fraction**

**Example:**
- 1 whole chapati + 1/2 chapati = **1 1/2**

### Practice
1. Identify the mixed fraction:
   - 3/4
   - 2 1/3 ✅
2. Draw and show 1 1/2

---

## 2. Average Learners
### Objective
- Convert improper fractions into mixed fractions

### Method
1. Divide numerator by denominator
2. Quotient = whole number
3. Remainder = numerator of fraction

**Example:**
7/3 = 2 1/3

### Practice
1. Convert to mixed fractions:
   - 5/2
   - 9/4

---

## 3. Moderate Learners
### Objective
- Convert both ways and solve simple word problems

### Formula
- Mixed to Improper:
  (Whole × Denominator + Numerator) / Denominator

**Example:**
3 2/5 = 17/5

### Practice
1. Convert:
   - 4 1/6
   - 13/4

---

## 4. Toppers
### Objective
- Deep understanding and application

### Higher Order Thinking
1. Which is greater: 2 3/4 or 11/4?
2. Write three improper fractions equal to 2 1/3

### Challenge
- A ribbon is 4 2/5 m long. How many 1/5 m pieces can be cut?
